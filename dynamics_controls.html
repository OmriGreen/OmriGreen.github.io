Robot Dynamics

Dynamics of Robotic Self-Assemblages

I simulated a robotic system inspired by the bridge-building behavior of army ants, using omni-directional robots as the platform. I built kinematic and dynamic models, implemented quintic trajectory generation for smooth motion, and tested the approach in Gazebo simulations. While I could not implement a functional multi-robot assembly, the project successfully demonstrated proof-of-concept dynamics for individual robots.

Robot Controls

Autonomous Underwater Vehicle Control

I worked on a team to design and simulate a control system for an underwater robot in MATLAB. The model accounted for buoyancy, drag, thrust, and environmental currents, with PD controllers managing orientation, position, and depth. We tested the robot on a multi-phase trajectory including descent and search patterns, showing that it could recover from initial errors, stay on path, and remain stable even under varying currents.
