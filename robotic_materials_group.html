Pneumatic Limiter

Soft One Way Valve Integrated into a Inflatable Life Preserver

Overview

According to the World Health Organization 236,000 fatalaties were estimated to occur by drowning per year, accounting for a full 7% of injuty related deaths. Additionally 75$ of all fatalaties in floods are due to drowning, in the 2022 1,922 people drowned due to seasonal floods in India alone. The problem is that typically first responders take a long time to search a flooded area for any potential survivors, the issue is that to save a drowning person they have only 3 minutes.

Approach

To address this issue we have developed the Pnuematic Limiter, a one way valve that can be attached to an inflatable life preserver that can be deployed with no additional hardware, when the . During testing it was shown that it would also automatically deploy when the pressure inside the life preserver was greater than the pressure outside, causing the limiter to be pushed out of the deployer.

Current Status

Sadly in November of 2021, the project lost funding, and I was unable to continue with the project. The plans were to develop a pnuematic multiplexer to allow a drone to carry up to 50 of these devices. However, testing did reveal that not only could the device self-deploy, but the inflated mock life preserver stayed inflated for 2 days.
